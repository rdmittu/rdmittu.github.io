About Me

My name is Rohan Mittu, and I am a student at Sherwood High School in Sandy Spring, MD. With passions for both computer science and biology, I take classes to persue and expand my knowledge of these sciences, along with working on projects either on my own, in a team at a hackathon, or in a professional setting.

My interest in computer science started when I was young. In my immediate family, my parents and three older siblings all persue computer science in their job or at school. Being in this enviornment my interest sparked, and I had access to learn anything from either online tutorials or asking questions. Beginning eigth grade, I decided to challenge myself to self-study for and take the AP Computer Science A Exam. Using AP material, online Java courses, and asking for help when I needed allowed me to excel learning the basics of computer programming, and score a "5" on the AP exam.

In my sophmore year of high school, I took AP Biology. Going into this class, I didn't quite know what to expect, but as time went on, I loved the time I spent taking notes and studing biology. I started to enjoy it so much that I read the textbook more including information not necessary for the class and biology articles and papers in my owntime. All the time spent studing biology put me ahead in the class, and to go on to recieving a "5" on the AP Biology Exam and a 800 on the SAT Microbiology Subject Test.
